Confidence Scoring

How Canon decides whether an asset is trustworthy or needs review

The Four Dimensions

📚 Evidence weight: 30%
Does the asset have evidence backing for what it teaches?
score = concepts_with_evidence / total_concepts_taught
🕐 Freshness weight: 30%
Was the asset built on current evidence?
score = fresh_evidence_links / total_evidence_links

fresh = evidence.last_verified ≤ asset.last_updated
🏗️ Structural weight: 20%
Are required ontology relationships present?
checks: concept? task? audience? evidence? objectives? prereqs?
score = checks_passed / total_checks
🤖 Transformation weight: 20%
How much AI risk was introduced?
dry_run / manual → 1.0  |  llm_single → 0.7  |  llm_adapted → 0.6  |  llm_multi → 0.5
Overall = (evidence × 0.30) + (freshness × 0.30) + (structural × 0.20) + (transformation × 0.20)

Key Properties

Proportional: One stale source is a warning. Multiple stale sources or a direct contradiction trigger review. The system doesn't cry wolf on every minor update.
Deterministic: Every score is a formula with inputs you can inspect. No magic numbers, no "I feel like this is a 0.4." You can always trace why a score is what it is.
Tunable: Weights (30/30/20/20), threshold (0.70), and transformation penalties are all configurable. Different teams might care more about freshness than structural completeness.
